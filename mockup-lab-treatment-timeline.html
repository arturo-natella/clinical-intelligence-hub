How Likelihood Is Calculated
Each side effect is scored against 5 evidence factors. The more factors present, the higher the confidence:

VERY HIGH 4-5 factors — Strong evidence of association. Multiple data sources align.
HIGH 3 factors — Likely associated with the medication. Known side effect with supporting timeline.
MODERATE 2 factors — Possibly related. Known side effect but other explanations exist.
LOW 0-1 factors — Unlikely to be medication-related, or insufficient evidence.

Evidence factors checked:
1. Known side effect — Is this reported in FDA adverse events (OpenFDA FAERS), drug labels (DailyMed), or clinical databases (SIDER)?
2. Temporal relationship — Did the symptom start after the medication was started or the dose was changed?
3. Dose-response — Did severity increase with a dose increase, or improve with a decrease?
4. Genetic factors — Does the patient's pharmacogenomic profile increase risk for this side effect? (PharmGKB, CPIC)
5. Alternative explanations — Can this symptom be explained by another medication, condition, or lifestyle factor?

This is a computational assessment for discussion purposes, not a clinical diagnosis. Always discuss medication concerns with your doctor.
